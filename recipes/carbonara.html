Pasta Carbonara

Description

Pasta Carbonara is one of the classic pasta dishes that originated from Rome. In Italian, the word carbone means charcoal, drawing similarity between the cracked black pepper scattered on on top of the pasta after garnishing. Nonetheless, carbonara lends itself as an incredibly simple and and inexpensive way to enjoy a traditional roman pasta dish today.

This recipe details how to prepare pasta carbonara in a more modern style using easily accessible and common cooking conventions found in most standard kitchens. In an elegant manner, pasta carbonara is considered an amalgamation eggs, hard cheese, cured pork and black pepper into a stand-out pasta dish served in a velvety, delicious sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 pound bacon (diced)
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • Kosher salt
  • Ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Place a large pot of water on the stove set to HIGH heat.
  2. As the water reaches a boil, salt the water and add the olive oil to a side sautee pan set to MED-HIGH heat.
  3. Add the bacon to the pan once the oil is heated and cook the bacon until crispy.
  4. Once crispy, kill the heat to the pan.
  5. Beat the eggs and roughly half the grated cheese into a mixing bowl.
  6. Add the dry pasta to the now rolling boil in the pot, cooking until al dente is reached.
  7. As the pasta finishes cooking, strain the pan of any residual oil and transfer the pasta from the pot to the pan.
  8. Reserve a small amount of pasta water from the pot, and toss the pasta and bacon in the pan.
  9. Add the beaten eggs and cheese mixture to the pasta, tossing frequently to prevent the eggs from settling.
  10. Add a small amount of pasta water to prevent the pasta from drying out, continuing to toss until sauce thickens.
  11. Add salt and pepper to taste, and enjoy!
