Honey Garlic Glazed Salmon

Description

As one of the more tender, delicate choices of fish available to consumers, salmon is an incredibly versatile meal that can be enjoyed in countless styles of preparation.

This recipe details how to prepare and sear a salmon filet on the stove in any large non-stick pan, as well as offers an excellent honey garlic glaze to enjoy the fish with. This recipe can be accompanied by countless sides, my favorites for this particular recipe being roasted petite potatoes, sauteed vegetables and/or steamed rice.

Ingredients

For the salmon...

  • 2 salmon filets (roughly 6 oz each)
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • (opt.) 1/8 teaspoon or dash blackening seasoning

For the glaze...

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Steps

  1. Pat salmon dry, then season with salt, pepper, paprika and blackening seasoning (opt.).
  2. Add butter and oil to large non-stick pan over MED-HIGH heat.
  3. Once butter melts, add garlic, water, soy sauce, sriracha, honey and lemon juice, cooking down for around 30 seconds.
  4. Add salmon (skin-side down) and cook for 3 minutes, basting frequently using the sauce from the pan.
  5. Remove filets to side and drain sauce from pan into a side heat-safe bowl.
  6. Re-add filets to pan over MED heat and sear for 4-5 minutes, until desired temperature is reached.
  7. Reduce the heat to LOW and re-add sauce to the pan, letting simmer for 1-2 minutes.
  8. Add any preferred garnishes and enjoy!
